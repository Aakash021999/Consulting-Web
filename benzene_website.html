Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What size of businesses do you work with?
+

We primarily work with MSMEs in the ₹5 crore to ₹500 crore revenue range. Large enough to have real operational complexity, yet agile enough to implement change quickly. We also work with supply chain divisions of larger corporates on project-specific engagements.

How long does a typical engagement last?
+

A diagnostic assessment typically takes 2–4 weeks. A full strategic planning engagement runs 6–12 weeks. Cost improvement and supply chain projects are usually 3–6 months. We also offer ongoing retainer arrangements for businesses that want continuous advisory support.

How do you charge — fixed fee or daily rate?
+

We offer both models. For well-defined projects, a fixed project fee provides budget certainty. For open-ended advisory, a monthly retainer or day-rate works better. We discuss this transparently during the discovery call — there are no surprises.

Do you work on-site or remotely?
+

Both. The diagnostic phase and key workshops are conducted on-site — direct observation is irreplaceable. Ongoing advisory, analysis, and reporting can be handled remotely. We're based in Delhi NCR but travel across India for engagements.

What results can I realistically expect?
+

Cost improvement projects typically deliver 15–30% reduction in targeted cost areas. Supply chain projects routinely cut inventory carrying costs by 20–40% and improve delivery reliability significantly. Strategic engagements build a clear 3-year roadmap — ROI materialises over 12–24 months.

How does your ERP advisory work?
+

We start with thorough process mapping before evaluating any ERP system. We create a vendor-neutral comparison across relevant platforms, facilitate structured demos, and score each option against your specific requirements. We have no referral arrangements with any ERP vendor — our recommendation is 100% objective.

Cost Transformation · Auto Components · Delhi NCR

₹8.4 Crore Saved for a Mid-Size Manufacturer

The Situation

A Delhi-based auto components manufacturer with ₹115Cr annual revenue had seen EBITDA margins compress from 14% to 8.3% over three years. The MD believed the issue was market pricing pressure. Our diagnosis told a different story.

What We Found

  • 187 active vendors — many duplicating each other, none with formal contracts or SLAs
  • Procurement decisions made by 6 different people with no central policy or approval matrix
  • Logistics costs 31% above industry benchmark due to poor load planning
  • ₹2.1Cr in annual overhead spend with no clear business justification (identified via Zero-Based Budgeting)
  • Energy consumption 18% above sector norms due to aging equipment and no consumption tracking
₹8.4Cr
Annual Savings
27%
Cost Reduction
4.2×
ROI on Fees

What We Did

  • Vendor rationalisation — reduced from 187 to 64 strategic vendors with formal contracts
  • Designed and implemented a centralised procurement policy with a 3-tier approval matrix
  • Renegotiated terms with top 15 vendors — average price reduction of 8.4%
  • Applied Zero-Based Budgeting across all overhead categories — eliminated ₹2.1Cr of unjustified spend
  • Redesigned logistics flow — consolidated loads reduced freight cost per unit from ₹42 to ₹29
  • Installed energy monitoring — identified and addressed top 5 consumption outliers

The Outcome

Within 14 weeks, ₹8.4Cr in annualised savings were identified and implementation was underway. EBITDA margin recovered from 8.3% to 14.7% within the following financial year. The client's CFO described it as "the highest-ROI initiative we've run in a decade."

Supply Chain Build · FMCG Trading · Pan-India

Building a Supply Chain from Zero in 20 Weeks

The Situation

A fast-growing FMCG trading company had scaled revenue from ₹12Cr to ₹48Cr in 3 years purely on founder relationships. But with no formal supply chain, growth was creating chaos — stockouts, delivery failures, supplier disputes, and a working capital crisis.

What Was Missing

  • No procurement policy, vendor master, or contract templates
  • No warehouse management system or layout optimisation
  • No demand forecasting — buying was entirely reactive
  • No distribution network — deliveries handled ad hoc via local transporters
  • 34 supplier payment disputes per month with no resolution process
20 wks

What We Built

  • Full procurement function — policy, vendor evaluation matrix, contract templates, payment terms
  • Warehouse redesign — ABC classification, location coding, pick-pack SOP, cycle count process
  • Demand planning model — rolling 8-week forecast using sales history and seasonality adjustments
  • Distribution network across 3 states — 2 regional 3PLs onboarded with SLA-backed contracts
  • ERP selection and implementation support — moved from Excel to a fit-for-purpose system
  • Supply chain performance dashboard — 8 KPIs tracked weekly by the MD

The Outcome

Six months post go-live: OTIF at 98.2%, inventory days reduced from 62 to 38, working capital freed by ₹3.3Cr, and the business successfully expanded into 2 new states. Revenue grew 38% in Year 1 — for the first time, supply chain was an enabler rather than a constraint.

Operational Turnaround · Regional 3PL · North India

From Loss-Making to EBITDA Positive in 18 Weeks

The Situation

A regional logistics company operating 60 vehicles across North India had posted losses for 3 consecutive quarters. The promoters were considering shutting down. A structured diagnosis revealed three fixable root causes — none of which required capital investment.

Root Causes Identified

  • Routes were on average 34% longer than optimal — no route optimisation had ever been done
  • Fleet utilisation at 61% — poor load planning meant vehicles running partially empty
  • Driver attrition at 48% annually — replacement and training cost was ₹80,000 per driver
  • No performance management system — drivers had no accountability for fuel or time
  • Billing disputes with 3 key clients causing ₹60L in delayed receivables
+22%
EBITDA Swing
₹3.1Cr
Cost Removed
18 wks
Duration

The Interventions

  • Route optimisation using GPS data — reduced average route length by 28%, saving ₹1.2Cr in fuel annually
  • Load consolidation algorithm — fleet utilisation improved from 61% to 92%
  • Driver incentive scheme redesigned — linked 20% of variable pay to fuel efficiency and on-time delivery
  • Attrition root cause analysis — addressed top 3 grievances; attrition fell from 48% to 22%
  • Billing dispute resolution — standardised proof-of-delivery process eliminated disputes entirely

The Outcome

The business swung from a quarterly loss of ₹42L to EBITDA positive within 18 weeks. Full-year annualised cost savings of ₹3.1Cr were locked in. The promoters, who had been considering exit, instead chose to expand the fleet by 15 vehicles in the following year.
